    How the calculation works:

    When you first sign up, you’ll start with a free balance of $300. After 3 days that initial $300 is returned, and your real compounding starts with a personal balance of $28.10.

    Your balance then grows by 1% per compounding period, and there are 3 compounding periods per day. Each 1% increase is rounded to the nearest tenth of a dollar before being added back to the balance.

    For example, if your balance is $28.10, a 1% increase is about $0.28, which rounds to $0.30 and gets added. The next period calculates 1% on the new total.

    This calculator uses the Start Date and Number of Days (or End Date) to determine the growth period. If you add withdrawals, they’re applied on the selected dates and the growth continues from the reduced balance.
